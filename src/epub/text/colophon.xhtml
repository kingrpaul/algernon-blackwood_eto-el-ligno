“A Bit of Wood” is a short story written by Algernon Blackwood, was first published in 1914 in Day and Night Stories, and is believed to be in the public domain. Digital scans can be found at the Internet Archive. A transcription was produced by “The Online Distributed Proofreading Team” and transcripts can be found at Project Gutenberg.

The work was translated from English to Esperanto as “Eto el Ligno” in 2024 by Paul King and that translation is released under the CC0 1.0 Universal (CC0 1.0) Public Domain Dedication.

The cover page was adapted from The Wood Sawyer by Charles E. Weir, and is believed to be in the public domain.

The cover and title pages feature the League Spartan and Sorts Mill Goudy typefaces created in 2014 and 2009 by The League of Moveable Type.
